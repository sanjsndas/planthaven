Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 1, 2023

This Cookie Policy explains how PlantHaven Care ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy.

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners. This policy describes what information they gather, how we use it, and why we sometimes need to store these cookies.

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners. Cookies are not harmful to your devices and cannot contain viruses or malware. They typically contain anonymous information such as a unique identifier, the site name, and some digits and numbers.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several purposes: to enable certain functions of our website, to provide analytics, to store your preferences, and to enable advertisements delivery. Specifically, we use cookies to remember your preferences, understand how our website is performing, and personalize content. Without certain types of cookies, some features on our site may not function properly. We may also use cookies to help us deliver targeted marketing campaigns.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We use both session cookies (which expire once you close your web browser) and persistent cookies (which stay on your device for a set period of time or until you delete them). The main types of cookies we use are: strictly necessary cookies (essential for website operation), performance cookies (collect information about how visitors use our site), functionality cookies (remember your preferences), and targeting cookies (used to deliver relevant ads).

4. First-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set by our website and can only be read by our site. These include cookies that are essential for the operation of our website, such as those that enable you to log into secure areas or use a shopping cart. We also use first-party cookies to remember your preferences, analyze how our site performs, and test different design ideas. These cookies help us provide you with a better user experience.

5. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are set by domains other than our website, typically by our partners and service providers. These include cookies used for analytics (like Google Analytics), advertising (like Google Ads), and social media integration (like Facebook buttons). These cookies may track your use of our website and other sites to provide targeted advertising or analyze user behavior. We don't control these cookies directly - you can manage them through your browser settings.

6. Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are necessary for our website to function properly. They enable basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas of the website. Without these cookies, the website cannot function properly. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information. Examples include cookies that maintain your session when you log in or remember items in your shopping cart during checkout.

7. Performance Cookies

Performance cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us know which pages are most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous.

8. Functionality Cookies

Functionality cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages. These cookies allow the website to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features. They may also be used to provide services you have asked for.

9. Targeting Cookies

Targeting cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant advertisements on other sites. They work by uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising. These cookies may track your browsing habits across websites.

10. Social Media Cookies

Social media cookies are set by a range of social media services that we have added to the site to enable you to share our content with your friends and networks. They are capable of tracking your browser across other sites and building up a profile of your interests. This may impact the content and messages you see on other websites you visit. If you do not allow these cookies, you may not be able to use or see these sharing tools.

11. Cookie Duration

The length of time a cookie stays on your device depends on whether it is a persistent or session cookie. Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain on your device until they expire or you delete them. The duration of persistent cookies varies - some expire after a few days, while others may remain for several years. You can manually delete cookies at any time through your browser settings.

12. Managing Cookies

You can control and/or delete cookies as you wish - for details, see aboutcookies.org. You can delete all cookies that are already on your computer and set most browsers to prevent them from being placed. However, if you do this, you may have to manually adjust some preferences every time you visit a site and some services and functionalities may not work. Most browsers allow you to refuse to accept cookies and to delete cookies.

13. Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow some control of most cookies through the browser settings. To find out more about cookies, including how to see what cookies have been set and how to manage and delete them, visit www.allaboutcookies.org. The Help menu on the menu bar of most browsers will tell you how to prevent your browser from accepting new cookies, how to have the browser notify you when you receive a new cookie, and how to disable cookies altogether.

14. Mobile Devices

If you access our website through a mobile device, you may need to refer to your device manufacturer's instructions to manage cookies. Many mobile devices have settings that allow you to control whether cookies are set or not. The process for managing cookies varies between different mobile operating systems and devices. You may need to consult your device's user manual or online help files for specific instructions.

15. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers incorporate a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Currently, our systems do not recognize browser "Do Not Track" signals. However, you can control tracking through cookies as described in this policy. We may update our practices regarding DNT signals as standards develop in this area.

16. Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to analyze the use of our website. Google Analytics gathers information about website use by means of cookies. The information gathered is used to create reports about the use of our website. Google's privacy policy is available at: https://policies.google.com/privacy. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

17. Facebook Pixel

We use Facebook Pixel to measure the effectiveness of our advertising and to show you relevant ads on Facebook. The Facebook Pixel is a piece of code that places a cookie on your device when you visit our site. This allows us to track conversions from Facebook ads, optimize ads, build targeted audiences for future ads, and remarket to people who have taken some kind of action on our website.

18. Advertising Cookies

We may use advertising cookies to deliver ads that we believe are relevant to you and your interests. These cookies remember that you visited our website and this information may be shared with other organizations such as advertisers. This means after you visit our site, you may see our advertisements on other websites. You can opt out of interest-based advertising by visiting the Digital Advertising Alliance's opt-out page.

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legislation, or our data use policies. When we change the policy in a material way, we will update the "last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to periodically review this page for the latest information on our cookie practices. Your continued use of our website after changes are made constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

22. Cookie Storage

Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser, while persistent cookies remain on your device until they expire or are deleted. You can manage cookie storage through your browser settings, allowing you to view, delete, or block cookies as needed.
