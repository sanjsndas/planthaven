Our Roots & Growth

From humble beginnings to becoming New York's premier organic gardening service

Rooted in Nature Since 1985

1985 - Humble Beginnings

Founded by horticulture enthusiasts Margaret and Robert Plant, PlantHaven started as a small urban gardening initiative in a Brooklyn community garden. Our first project transformed a vacant lot into a thriving vegetable garden for local residents.

1995 - Expanding Horizons

After a decade of successful community projects, we expanded to residential services, helping homeowners create sustainable, beautiful gardens. This period saw the development of our signature organic soil amendment techniques.

2010 - Professional Recognition

Our work received the New York Sustainable Landscaping Award, recognizing our innovative approaches to urban organic gardening. We expanded our team to include certified arborists and landscape architects.

Present Day - Continuing the Mission

Today, PlantHaven Care serves the entire tri-state area with a team of 15 expert consultants. We remain committed to our founding principles while incorporating modern sustainable practices.

Expert Gardening Tips

Practical advice from our decades of organic gardening experience

Soil Preparation

Healthy soil is the foundation of any garden. Test your soil pH and amend it with organic compost at least 2 weeks before planting. For every square foot, mix in 2-3 inches of compost to improve texture and nutrient content.

Proper Pruning

When pruning trees, always cut just outside the branch collar at a 45-degree angle. The best time to prune most trees is late winter when they're dormant. Remove no more than 25% of the canopy in a single season.

Read more →

Watering Wisdom

Water deeply but infrequently to encourage deep root growth. Early morning is the best time to water, reducing evaporation and fungal growth. A general rule is 1 inch of water per week, including rainfall.

Read more →

Natural Pest Control

Encourage beneficial insects by planting nectar-rich flowers. For aphids, try a strong spray of water or insecticidal soap. Companion planting with marigolds or garlic can deter many common pests.

Read more →

Seasonal Planting

Understand your USDA hardiness zone and plant accordingly. Cool-season crops (lettuce, peas) thrive in spring and fall, while warm-season crops (tomatoes, peppers) need summer heat. Rotate crops annually to prevent soil depletion.

Read more →

Composting Basics

Maintain a 3:1 ratio of browns (carbon-rich materials like leaves) to greens (nitrogen-rich materials like vegetable scraps). Turn your compost pile weekly and keep it moist like a wrung-out sponge. In 3-6 months, you'll have rich garden gold!
